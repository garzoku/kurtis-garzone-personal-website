Creative Process

How I write a story

As a writer of fiction, I have written countless stories. Most of them end up in a waste basket while some go on to become completed works that I can feel proud of. Every story starts with an idea, and the inherent problem with this is that, at first, every idea seems like a good one. I've developed a 6-step process for writing that helps me sort the good stories from the bad ones. Here it is.

Step 1
Once you've thought of your story idea, write the first draft and do not pause to go back and read itâ€”No matter how tempted you may be!
Step 2
In the case that you print your rough drafts like I do, put the unread draft in a cabinet, drawer, or even a steel safe if that's what it takes to keep your eyes off it for at least two weeks, preferrably one month. If it's digital, put it in a folder somewhere you'll not access it.
Step 3
After at least two weeks have passed, you'll have forgotten most of what you had written. You may now read your story for the first time with a fresh pair of eyes.
Step 4
Since the story is mostly unfamiliar to you, you should quickly be able to recognize whether or not the story's plot, characters, and other details show signs of potential.
Step 5
You should be able to decide now if this story is flat-out bad, or if it shows signs of potential (after another draft, of course. The first draft is typically only good enough if you are a true story-telling genius).
Step 6
For your second draft, rinse and repeat until you find the story meets your expectations. Then celebrate! You should have a well-rounded story!
